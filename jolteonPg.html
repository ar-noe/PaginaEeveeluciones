JOLTEON

Jolteon (Japanese: サンダース, Hepburn: Sandaasu) (JOL-tee-on[1]) is an Electric-type Pokémon introduced in Generation I. It is one of the eight final forms of Eevee, as a result by using a Thunder Stone.

Jolteon is a quadrupedal, mammalian, feliform Pokémon, covered in spiny bright yellow fur. It has long ears resembling a hare or jackrabbit, deep purple (or black) almond-shaped eyes, and a blunt catlike muzzle, with a small, black triangular nose. It has a spiky white mane encircling its neck. Its legs and paws are slender, with three toes on each and no visible claws. Jolteon has a spiny fringe of fur covering its backside, and a small short spike-like tail that is not often visible (but seen in games such as Pokémon Stadium). Its back legs seem to be powerfully-built, allowing it to run at high speeds, like a cheetah and a hare, as it resembles both fast animals. In Pokémon card art, it is shown to be leaping upward like a hare, with its long feet extended.

A Shiny Jolteon's fur is a bright, neon-shade of chartreuse. The rest of its body is the same colour as its non-shiny form.
